It's easy to get engaged with your city

Adopt-a-thon↓

A web app that encourages citizens to "adopt" civic infrastructure, such as fire hydrants, storm drains, trees, emergency sirens...anything, really. Adopta has already been deployed in 4 cities -- your city can be next.

Your objective is to maximize adoptions and see them through when action is needed. This might be a good app for you if:

  • You have a dataset you want to use.
  • You have Ruby on Rails expertise.

Write-a-thon ↓

A platform for building community information hubs. LocalWikis are wikis about a place written by the people of that place.

This might be a good app for you if: You have a local group who wants to be involved to help seed content. You like community organizing. Your objective is to maximize contributions and links to your wiki.

  • Launch: It's trivial. Just send email to LocalWiki & let them know you want an instance for the Code for America campaign.
  • Make Useful: You'll need to invest time recruiting authors and curators. Take a look at how they did it at TriangleWiki
