The Competition

Build Locally, Spread Nationally: A New Kind of Apps Contest

Across the county, civic hackers have been building amazing applications to address real needs in their communities -- and because they are good people like you, they’ve made it freely available for anyone else to reuse.

So now we’re challenging you to step up.

Choose an application from our growing catalog and get a team together to deploy it in your city — and then the real competition starts: you’ll be racing against teams across the country to drive adoption, build a user base, and create real impact in your community.

How it Works

Gather a team.
Register by October 15th

Redeploy.
 Launch one or more of the featured apps to your city or any city.

Create engagement.
Deploying an app is just the first milestone. This competition is about engaging citizens to participate in activities that apps support.

Unlock achievements.
There are multiple rewards along the way. You'll send us proof of reaching milestones and we'll send you the rewards.

Rewards

A prize is something you win for showing up. A reward is something you earn for what you do.

Achievements

Launch your app and earn an advertising budget to promote it.

Fulfill you app challenge for adoption and earn a year of hosting.

Launch more than one app or deploy in more than one city and we'll host your next 3 Brigade Meetups (or comparable event).

Reach all achievements by November 6, and we'll throw you a party. (Actually, we'll give your $500 to throw your own party.)

Rules

  1. Registration. Registration is required to be eligible for rewards. Online registration ends at 10:00 pm Pacific Time on October 15, 2012. We encourage you to register early to get maximum exposure for your effort.
  2. Cost. There is no cost for entering the competition.
  3. Start & End Times. Teams may begin racing toward milestones immediately upon verification of entry. Verification will be delivered by email to the address provided upon registrations. The competition ends at 10 pm Pacific Time on November 6, 2012.
  4. Press Release. By participating in Code for America competitions, participants grant organizers and participating members of the media rights to publicly disclose competition event information, review and describe applications developed and presented during the event, and rights to display event-related photos, screen shots, and links to publicly available demonstrations of contest entries.
  5. Verification. Competitors are required to submit proof for each milestones reached, ex., the URL of an app site for reaching the deploy milestone, a screen or other verification showing the number of adoptions, and the like. We apply the honor code to all verification. Any misconduct around inflating your analytics AKA “juking the stats” is grounds for expulsion from the competition.
  6. Rules Changes. Code for America reserves the right to make rules modifications prior to the start of the event.

Apps

Textizen

A simple, powerful citizen feedback tool for the digital age, with SMS. This might be a good app for you if: You are a city official or have contacts in your local government. You don’t have a technical background . You have a specific question(s) or project you want to get feedback on from your community.

Level of Effort

  • Launch: It's trivial. Just send email to Textizen & let them know you want an instance for the Code for America campaign.
  • Make Useful: You'll need to invest time making and promoting a poll that's relevant in your city.

Get started with Textizen

You’ll be competing to get the greatest number of survey respondents.

LocalWiki

A platform for building collaborative community information hubs. This might be a good app for you if: You have a local group who wants to be involved to help seed content. You like community organizing.

Level of Effort

  • Launch: It's trivial. Just send email to LocalWiki & let them know you want an instance for the Code for America campaign.
  • Make Useful: You'll need to invest time recruiting authors and curators. Take a look at how they did it at TriangleWiki

Get started with LocalWiki

You’ll be competing to get the greatest number of unique contributors to your wiki.

Adopta

A Ruby on Rails application that allows citizens to "adopt" civic infrastructure, such as fire hydrants, storm drains, trees, emergency sirens...anything, really. This might be a good app for you if: You have Ruby on Rails expertise. You have a dataset you want to use.

Level of Effort

  • Launch: You'll need the locations of fire hydrants or whatever it is your app can offer for adoption. You can deploy it Heroku for free.
  • Make Useful: You'll need to invest time recruiting adopters. Check out these no-tech adopt-a-hydrant programs at fire departments across the country.

Get started with Adopta

You’ll be competing to get the highest percentage of your adoptable items adopted.

Answers

A Ruby on Rails application that presents a "great big search box" as an interface to your city's web site.

Level of Effort

  • Launch: You'll need to fork the code and ... You can deploy it to Heroku for free.
  • Make Useful: You'll need to invest time recruiting authors and curators. Take a look at how they did it at Honolulu Answers

Get started with Answers

You’ll be competing to answer the most questions.
