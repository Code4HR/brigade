The Campaign

We're asking Brigade members across the country to help their communities stand up civic apps. Then, together, we'll campaign in those communities to boot up civic engagement online.

How it Works

Redeploy.
 Launch one or more of the featured apps to your city or any city.

Enter.
Submit an app URL
by Election Day, November 6th.

Create engagement.
This campaign is about engaging citizens through activities that apps support. We'll share our megaphone with your and help you spread the word.

Unlock achievements.
There are multiple rewards along the way. Share your proof of reaching milestones and we'll send you the rewards.

Rewards

Launch your app by Election Day and earn an advertising budget to promote it.

Fulfill you app challenge for adoption and earn a year of hosting.

Launch more than one app or deploy in more than one city and we'll supply your next 6 Brigade Meetups.

Reach all achievements by Thanksgiving Day 2012, and we'll throw you a party. (Actually, we'll give your $500 to throw your own party.)

Your objective is to organize a poll for your city and publish results in public.

A feedback tool for the mobile age, with SMS. This might be a good app for you if: You are a city official or have contacts in your local government. You donâ€™t have a technical background. You have a specific question(s) or project for which you want feedback from your community.

Level of Effort

  • Launch: It's trivial. Fill out the "Textizen in your city" form on www.textizen.com. Let them know you are participating in the Code for America App Raiser campaign.
  • Make Useful: You'll need to invest time making and promoting a poll that's relevant in your city.

Get started with Textizen

LocalWiki

Your objective is to maximize contributions and links to your wiki.

A platform for building community information hubs. LocalWikis are wikis about a place written by the people of that place. This might be a good app for you if: You have a local group who wants to be involved to help seed content. You like community organizing.

Level of Effort

  • Launch: It's trivial. Just send email to LocalWiki & let them know you want an instance for the Code for America campaign.
  • Make Useful: You'll need to invest time recruiting authors and curators. Take a look at how they did it at TriangleWiki

Get started with LocalWiki

Adopta

Your objective is to maximize adoptions and see them through when action is needed.

A web app that encourages citizens to "adopt" civic infrastructure, such as fire hydrants, storm drains, trees, emergency sirens...anything, really. This might be a good app for you if: You have Ruby on Rails expertise. You have a dataset you want to use.

Level of Effort

  • Launch: You'll need the locations of fire hydrants or whatever it is your app can offer for adoption. You can deploy it to Heroku for free.
  • Make Useful: You'll need to invest time recruiting adopters. Check out these no-tech adopt-a-hydrant programs at fire departments across the country.

Get started with Adopta
