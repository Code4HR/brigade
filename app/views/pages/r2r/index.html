The Competition

Build Locally, Spread Nationally: A New Kind of Apps Contest

Across the county, civic hackers have been building amazing applications to address real needs in their communities -- and because they are good people like you, they’ve made it freely available for anyone else to reuse.

So now we’re challenging you to step up.

Choose an application from our growing catalog and get a team together to deploy it in your city — and then the real competition starts: you’ll be racing against teams across the country to drive adoption, build a user base, and create real impact in your community.

Rules

  1. The Race for Reuse will run from October 3rd to November 6 (?), 2012.
  2. You can stand up your app before the October 3rd start date if you want to hit the ground running, but only user metrics achieved during the time period from October 3rd to November 6th will be counted.
  3. We will ask you to submit progress updates (in the form of screenshots) every two weeks.
  4. We apply the honor code to reporting of stats. Any misconduct around inflating your analytics AKA “juking the stats” is grounds for expulsion from the competition and shunning within the civic hacker community.

Rewards

A prize is something you win for showing up. A reward is something you earn for what you do.

Achievements

Stand up your app and earn an advertising budget.

Fulfill you app challenge for adoption and earn a year of hosting.

Launch more than one app or deploy in more than one city and we'll host your next 3 Brigade Meetups (or comparable event).

Apps

Textizen

A simple, powerful citizen feedback tool for the digital age, with SMS. This might be a good app for you if: You are a city official or have contacts in your local government. You don’t have a technical background . You have a specific question(s) or project you want to get feedback on from your community.

Get started with Textizen

You’ll be competing to get the greatest number of survey respondents.

LocalWiki

A platform for building collaborative community information hubs. This might be a good app for you if: You have a local group who wants to be involved to help seed content. You like community organizing.

Get started with LocalWiki

You’ll be competing to get the greatest number of unique contributors to your wiki.

Adopta

A Ruby on Rails application that allows citizens to "adopt" civic infrastructure, such as fire hydrants, storm drains, or emergency sirens.. This might be a good app for you if: You have Ruby on Rails expertise. You have a dataset you want to use.

Get started with Adopta

You’ll be competing to get the highest percentage of your adoptable items adopted.
